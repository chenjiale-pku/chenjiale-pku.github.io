Biography

I am a first-year Ph.D. student in operations research at Stanford University, rotating with Prof. Amin Saberi and Prof. Aaron Sidford . Previously, I obtained my B.Sc., Summa Cum Laude in Computer Science and Technology from Turing Class, Peking University, advised by Prof. Yuqing Kong . During my undergraduate years, I am fortunate to have worked closely with Prof. Ariel Procaccia at Harvard University and Prof. Jason Hartline at Northwestern University.

My research interests lie in the intersection of theoretical computer science and economics, with a goal to provide elegant explanations and practical guidance for social phenomena. Recently I’ve been exploring topics in graph algorithms.
